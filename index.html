Open-World Robotic Control

Techniques, Challenges, and Applications
RSS 2025, Los Angeles, USA

Overview

Open-world robotics stands as a critical frontier in the field, essential for bridging the gap between controlled laboratory demonstrations and impactful real-world deployments. This workshop directly addresses this crucial need by focusing on the latest advancements in both control-centric and reasoning-based approaches for enabling robust and adaptable robot operation in unstructured, dynamic environments. This represents a significant departure from traditional robotics research, which often relies on simplified assumptions and structured settings.

The contributed content is expected to reflect the state of the art by showcasing cutting-edge research across several key areas. This includes novel control strategies for dexterous manipulation in the wild, enabling robots to interact with complex and unpredictable objects and environments; advancements in open-world navigation and autonomous driving, tackling challenges like long-range planning, dynamic obstacle avoidance, and adaptation to unforeseen scenarios; and the burgeoning field of leveraging foundation models for robotics, exploring how large language models and multimodal representations can enhance perception, reasoning, and long-horizon decision-making in robots. We anticipate contributions exploring innovative methods for learning from diverse data sources, including vision, language, and real-world interactions, to build more adaptable and generalizable robot systems.

Furthermore, by explicitly encouraging submissions on up-and-coming topics like open-vocabulary learning and VLA foundation models, we aim to expand the originality and diversity of ICRA 2025's workshop content. This focus on pushing the boundaries of current research will stimulate new ideas and facilitate exploration of unconventional approaches.

The confluence of recent advancements in robust control, particularly in learning-based and adaptive control methods, and the increasing accessibility of powerful foundation models makes this workshop particularly timely. It provides a unique and vital platform for cross-disciplinary dialogue and knowledge exchange between researchers from diverse fields, including control theory, embodied AI, computer vision, natural language processing, and large language models for robotics. This interdisciplinary exchange is crucial for fostering collaborations, sparking innovation, and accelerating progress towards truly capable and robust open-world robotic systems. The workshop will serve as a catalyst for pushing the boundaries of robotics research and enabling wider real-world deployment across various impactful applications, ranging from autonomous driving and exploration to assistive robotics and disaster relief. We anticipate this workshop will contribute significantly to shaping the future of robotics and accelerating the transition towards widespread real-world applications.
